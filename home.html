Model for Change:
How Sport Can Prevent
Sexual and Domestic Violence

Much of what sport does, teaches, and promotes is congruent with sexual and domestic violence prevention efforts. This Model for Change articulates how individual and organizations in sport can utilize current values, codes of conduct, and cultural norms across the sport pipeline to build toward a common vision.

This Model for Change articulates a framework for prevention whereby sport is activated as an avenue and platform to end sexual and domestic violence.

What is it?

The Model for Change is comprehensive description and illustration of how and why a desired change (ending domestic and sexual violence) is expected to happen in a particular context (in and through sport). It maps out the steps between what a program or change initiative does (its activities or interventions) and how these lead to the desired impact (reducing or eliminating SV/DV in and through sport).

It is a roadmap that can lead the sport and SV/DV advocacy community from the problem (current state) to the solution (visionary goal).

How we developed it?

First, we identified the problem and conducted research (See OVERVIEW REPORT) to better understand the problem and existing interventions. In this research, we also refined the long-term goals with both the sport community and SV/DV advocacy community. Once we solidified the desired long-term goals, we then worked back from these to identify all the conditions (outcomes) that must be in place for the goals to occur.Â 

The short, medium and long term outcomes in the Model for Change provide a basis for identifying what type of activity or intervention will lead to the outcomes identified as preconditions for achieving the long-term goal. Through this approach the precise link between activities and the achievement of the long-term goals are more fully understood. This leads to better planning, in that activities are linked to a detailed understanding of how change actually happens. It also leads to better evaluation, as it is possible to measure progress towards the achievement of longer-term goals that goes beyond the identification of program outputs.
